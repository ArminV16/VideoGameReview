Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice Review

Story - 7/10

Sekiro's story follows the journey of **Shinobi Wolf**, a warrior on a mission to rescue his kidnapped lord. While the plot offers a compelling revenge story with themes of sacrifice and honor, it doesn't dive as deep as other narratives in action games. The story is engaging but feels somewhat linear at times, with limited character development beyond the protagonist.

Gameplay - 10/10

The gameplay of Sekiro is exceptional, offering intense swordplay that requires precision, timing, and strategy. The posture-based combat system is incredibly satisfying, with each encounter feeling both challenging and rewarding. The addition of stealth mechanics, exploration, and a variety of tools (such as the grappling hook) creates a dynamic and engaging experience that sets it apart from other action games.

Graphics & Scenery - 10/10

Sekiro's world is a visual masterpiece. The game features stunning landscapes, from lush forests to ominous castles and eerie temples. The attention to detail is breathtaking, and the scenery is both beautiful and atmospheric. Whether you're scaling a mountain or exploring a war-torn battlefield, the world feels alive, immersive, and meticulously crafted. The dynamic lighting and weather further enhance the experience, making each area feel unique and impactful.

Bosses - 9/10

The bosses in Sekiro are some of the most memorable and challenging in recent gaming history. Each boss has its own distinct moveset and unique abilities that require players to learn, adapt, and master the combat mechanics. Some bosses are incredibly tough and will test your patience, but the sense of achievement after defeating them is unmatched. The only downside is that a few bosses can feel repetitive in their design or tactics.

Final rating 9/10

Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice is a masterpiece in many aspects. While its story might not stand out as much as its gameplay and visuals, it offers one of the most intense and rewarding experiences in modern gaming. Whether you're a fan of challenging combat or breathtaking world design, Sekiro is an unforgettable journey.
